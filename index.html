About

Finger Lakes Distilling

Finger Lakes Distilling is the region's first stand-alone craft distillery — established in Burdett, NY in 2007 by Brian McKenzie and Thomas Earl McKenzie, two men who met at a craft distiller's conference and discovered a shared passion for high-quality handmade spirits. Neither finance background nor farm background alone would have been enough. Together, they built something that's become a landmark of the Finger Lakes.

As a New York State Farm Distillery, every spirit starts with locally grown fruit and grains. Two copper stills — a 350-gallon German-made pot still and a 25-foot continuous still from Vendome Copper and Brass Works in Kentucky — do the work. The lineup spans McKenzie Whiskey (bourbon, rye, and bottled-in-bond), Seneca Drums Gin, Vintner's Vodka made from Finger Lakes grapes, brandy, grappa, and a range of liqueurs. No extracts, no synthetic flavorings — just real ingredients and time-honored technique.

The tasting room overlooks the production floor for an educational and immersive experience. The Tippling House serves cocktails and light bites with deck seating and sweeping Seneca Lake views. And for the truly hands-on visitor, the make-your-own-gin workshop lets groups of up to 8 distill and bottle their own custom blend.

In Our Words

Our Story

Finger Lakes Distilling is the real deal — and has been since before craft distilling was a thing people planned road trips around. The McKenzie Bourbon regularly surprises people who come in expecting a regional novelty and leave reconsidering what they thought they knew about American whiskey. The rye is exceptional. The Vintner's Vodka — distilled from actual Finger Lakes grapes — is unlike any vodka you've tasted.

The building alone is worth the stop. That Scottish distillery-inspired architecture with the pagoda roofline sitting above Seneca Lake is one of the most distinctive views on Route 414. If you have any interest at all in how spirits are made, the production floor view from the tasting room is genuinely fascinating. And if you want to go deeper, the make-your-own-gin workshop is one of the most memorable experiences in the entire region.

— South East Seneca

The Spirits

What's in the Glass

All spirits are handcrafted on-site using two copper stills and locally sourced ingredients. Tasting flights available — ask about current seasonal and limited releases.

  • McKenzie Bourbon ★Sweet mash bourbon aged in new charred oak — consistently surprises whiskey veterans
  • McKenzie RyeEmpire Rye style — part of a NY State effort to revive pre-Prohibition rye heritage
  • Bottled-in-Bond BourbonSingle distillery, single season, aged 4+ years at 100 proof — a real achievement
  • Seneca Drums Gin ★Named NY's Best Spirit — locally sourced botanicals, clean and distinctive
  • Vintner's VodkaDistilled from Finger Lakes grapes — has the character of the wine it came from
  • Brandy, Grappa & LiqueursPear, apple, and grape brandies plus a range of fruit liqueurs — all made with real local fruit

Experiences

More Than a Tasting

Finger Lakes Distilling offers experiences you won't find anywhere else on the Seneca Lake trail.

Make Your Own Gin Workshop

Designed for groups of 1–8. Choose your botanicals, create your own blend, distill it on-site, and take home a bottle of your own gin. One of the most unique experiences in the Finger Lakes. Reservations required.

The Tippling House

A dedicated cocktail bar and light food menu with outdoor deck seating overlooking Seneca Lake. Handcrafted cocktails featuring Finger Lakes Distilling spirits, plus local beer and wine. Open during regular tasting room hours.

Production Floor Views

The tasting room is designed so visitors can see directly into the production area. Watch the stills, ask questions, and understand how each spirit gets made — a genuinely educational experience.

Special Events

Derby parties, dinner pairings with local restaurants, seasonal releases, and more throughout the year. Follow on Facebook for the latest event announcements.

Plan Your Visit

Tips for Visitors

  • Start with the McKenzie Bourbon — it regularly surprises people who think they know American whiskey.
  • Don't skip the Tippling House — the deck view of Seneca Lake is one of the best on Route 414.
  • Book the make-your-own-gin workshop ahead of time — it fills up fast and is limited to 8 people.
  • Groups of 12 or more require a reservation — call ahead at (607) 546-5510.
  • Open 7 days a week — Thursday through Saturday they stay open until 8 PM.
  • Dogs are welcome — bring them along for the visit.
  • About 5 miles north of Watkins Glen on Route 414 — easy to pair with a state park visit.
